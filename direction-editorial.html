The Front Page · Profile

Proof that untreated curiosity can become a career.

By the desk of A. Reiss  |  Filed from the California desert

For three decades the work of Andi Reiss has refused to sit still. A media producer who became a documentarian, who became a journalist, an author, a screenwriter, a photographer, a commercial drone pilot, an editor, a sound designer, an illustrator and an educator — not by indecision, but by appetite.

The feature drama Cargo premiered at Cannes in 2004 and opened the Taormina Film Festival. The films that followed travelled to Slamdance, SXSW, Raindance, Telluride and beyond. The throughline was never the festival laurel; it was the insistence on telling true things plainly.

Now based in Palm Springs, he runs a one-person production ecosystem — writing, shooting, flying, cutting and scoring — while drafting a 600-page travelogue and a PhD proposal on how the screens in our pockets are quietly rewriting the world.

“A raw and honest approach that stands out — emphasising truth over artifice.”

— James Christopher, The Guardian
